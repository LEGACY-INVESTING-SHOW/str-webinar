##### Slide #27

Location.

Is the market active at least 7 or more months a year? That's the seasonality factor. Some places, like Park City, Utah, boom in winter but slow down in summer. I'm not saying avoid seasonal markets. You can still win with a top property. But focus on annual income, that's what really matters.

Does the area have attractions? Beaches, tourist spots, hospitals, or big businesses nearby are all good signs.

Is there population growth? Are people moving into this area or leaving? Growing cities have rising demand. Shrinking cities have falling rents and property values.

What about the job market? Is the economy diverse or dependent on one employer? Tech hubs, medical centers, and cities with multiple industries are safer bets than single-factory towns.

And can you create a memorable guest experience? Maybe it's near a lake, has a pool, or vaulted ceilings. Anything that stands out in photos and makes someone stop scrolling.

##### Slide #31

Step 4. Be conservative and run your numbers.

AirDNA shows averages. You're new. No reviews yet. Assume you'll underperform in year one. If AirDNA says 70% occupancy, use 60%. If AirDNA says $65,000 revenue, use $55,000.

If worst case still makes money, the deal is worth pursuing. If worst case loses money, walk away.

The best way to reduce fear is more data. Most of the anxiety people feel about this comes from not knowing the numbers. When you can see on a screen that worst case you still make money, the fear goes away. You make decisions based on data instead of drama.

##### Slide #52

**On-slide: Strategy 1 — The STR Loophole**

This is one of the biggest tax advantages in real estate, and it works specifically with short-term rentals.

The IRS treats short-term rentals with an average guest stay under 7 days as an active business, not a passive one. If your Airbnb shows a paper loss from depreciation, furniture, or startup costs, you can deduct that loss against your W-2 income directly.

Qualification: actively participate in management, roughly 100 hours a year or more than anyone else. That's about 2 hours a week.

This is the ONLY way a W-2 earner can offset their active income with real estate losses without qualifying as a full-time real estate professional.

---

##### Slide #53

Here's the example. You make $200K from your job. Your Airbnb shows a $50K paper loss after deductions and depreciation. You subtract $50K from your taxable income. That saves $15K to $20K in taxes depending on your bracket.

These are paper deductions. You're not losing real money. You're lowering your taxable income while keeping your cash flow intact.

This is only available through ownership. Bridge gives you cash flow, but you're taxed on every dollar. Ownership gives you cash flow PLUS paper losses that reduce your W-2 taxes.

I'm not a CPA and this isn't tax advice. Every situation is different. But this is a strategy our CPA partners implement for clients regularly.

---

##### Slide #54

**On-slide: Strategy 2 — Cost Segregation + Bonus Depreciation**

Now pair that with Cost Segregation and Bonus Depreciation.

Cost segregation means breaking your property into smaller components instead of treating it as one long-term asset. Different parts wear out at different speeds, so the IRS lets you depreciate them faster.

Items that get separated out: furniture, appliances, lighting fixtures, flooring, cabinetry, landscaping, outdoor structures.

Once those short-life items are identified, Bonus Depreciation lets you deduct a large percentage immediately in year one.

Thanks to the One Big Beautiful Bill Act signed in July 2025, bonus depreciation is permanently at 100% for qualifying property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025. Full write-off, year one.

You could go from paying $50,000 in taxes to under $5,000. That's potentially $45,000 to $55,000 back in your pocket. In one year. While the property also generates cash flow.
