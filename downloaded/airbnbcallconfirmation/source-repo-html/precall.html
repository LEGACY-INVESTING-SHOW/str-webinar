What We Learned

What We Figured Out The Hard Way So You Don't Have To.

Almost everyone assumes the danger in this business is on the market side. Bad property, rate hikes, recession. After watching thousands of operators run this play, market risk is rarely what takes anyone out. Execution is.

Here's what actually takes operators out:

  • LLC set up in the wrong state. Zero tax savings plus thousands in compliance fees.
  • Properties held under personal name instead of an entity. One lawsuit on one property can reach every other one they own.
  • Pricing off a comp from the wrong neighborhood. Thousands a month left on the table without realizing it.
  • Missing the cost segregation window in year one. Lose the biggest tax win the property was ever going to produce.

None of these show up in YouTube tutorials. They show up after the ink is dry, and by then it's too late to undo.

Inside the program, those execution gaps are handled by people who've already run the version that worked. Every deal gets reviewed. Every entity gets set up correctly. Every cost seg gets timed right. The team has run this play hundreds of times, which is why the results repeat.

Bridge Method or Ownership. How do I know which one is right for me?

Both routes work. Your advisor figures out which one fits your situation on the call.

Bridge Method is the right move if you want to build cash flow fast without tying up $50K to $150K in a down payment, you're comfortable starting without a property in your name, and your goal in the next 12 months is replacing or supplementing income, not maximizing tax savings.

Typical numbers: $10K to $25K to launch your first unit. Around $2,500 a month in net profit per property. Three of them stacked puts you at roughly $7,500 a month alongside your day job. Most Bridge Method clients then graduate to Ownership inside 18 to 24 months.

Ownership is the right move if your tax bill is the biggest leak in your finances, you have $50K to $150K of capital available for a down payment, and you're a high earner ($150K+) where cost segregation makes the real difference.

Typical numbers on a $500K to $750K property: $23K+ in year-one cash flow, $55K to $75K in year-one tax savings through the STR loophole and 100% bonus depreciation, plus equity. For a high earner, the tax savings on a single property can exceed the down payment itself.
