Bridge Method or Ownership. How do I know which one is right for me?

Both routes work. Your advisor figures out which one fits your situation on the call.

Bridge Method is the right move if you want to build cash flow fast without tying up $50K to $150K in a down payment, you're comfortable starting without a property in your name, and your goal in the next 12 months is replacing or supplementing income, not maximizing tax savings.

Typical numbers: $10K to $25K to launch your first unit. Around $2,500 a month in net profit per property. Three of them stacked puts you at roughly $7,500 a month alongside your day job. Most Bridge Method clients then graduate to Ownership inside 18 to 24 months, once they've built the cash flow and operational reps.

Ownership is the right move if your tax bill is the biggest leak in your finances, you have $50K to $150K of capital available for a down payment, and you're a high earner ($150K+) where cost segregation makes the real difference.

Typical numbers on a $500K to $750K property: $23K+ in year-one cash flow, $55K to $75K in year-one tax savings through the STR loophole and 100% bonus depreciation, plus equity. For a high earner, the tax savings on a single property can exceed the down payment itself.

Bring honest numbers to the call. Your capital, tax bracket, and time horizon. Your advisor will walk you through which route the math actually supports for you right now.

Does the wealth foundation apply if I'm just a W-2 earner?

That's exactly who it was built for. Many of our biggest results come from people who walked in with one job, no business, and no real estate yet.

The five pillars work for W-2 earners the same way they work for self-employed people:

  1. Tax optimization. Strategies that work without owning a business.
  2. Debt and credit leverage. Restructure high-interest debt, access 0% business credit on a new LLC.
  3. Income acceleration. Strategies to add cash flow alongside your existing job.
  4. Savings and investing. What to actually do with capital so it grows.
  5. Entity structure and protection. LLC setup, trust planning, asset protection.

Even before you launch your first STR, the foundation does real work for you. Specific tax wins available to W-2 earners right now:

  • 401(k) or Solo 401(k) maxed out: ~$4,004 a year in tax savings
  • IRA contributions with backdoor Roth strategy: ~$1,540 a year
  • HSA properly used (triple-tax advantage): ~$913 a year
  • STR loophole (once you have your first property): $55,000 to $75,000 a year on a $500K-$750K property

That's $6,500+ a year in W-2-side tax wins available before you own a single STR. Once you do, the STR loophole layered on top compounds the result fast.

Most W-2 earners feel stuck because nobody ever showed them how to keep more of what they're already making. The program does that, then layers the STR side on top.
