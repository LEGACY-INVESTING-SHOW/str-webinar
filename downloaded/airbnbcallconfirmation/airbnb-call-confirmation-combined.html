Routes

Pick the route before you spend money.

Most people come in thinking there is one Airbnb path. The call sorts the route before you spend money: lower-capital Bridge, ownership, or Concierge if you want more of the acquisition and setup handled.

Bridge

Lease first, keep startup capital lower.

Bridge uses rental arbitrage: lease a property, furnish it, list it, and keep the spread after expenses.

  • Typical launch capital: $10K to $25K
  • Team support on landlord outreach, deal review, scripts, roleplay, and references
  • Common target: around $2,500/month net cash flow when the deal and execution line up
Ownership

Buy the asset and build the tax/equity side.

Ownership is for people who want the property in their name and want cash flow, equity, depreciation, and more control.

  • Typical capital range: $50K to $150K, depending on the property and loan structure
  • Concierge support can include buy box, sourcing, underwriting, lender and CPA intros, and launch setup
  • Best fit when tax strategy and long-term ownership matter as much as monthly cash flow

Rather read?

The questions everyone asks before the call.

I've never done real estate. Can I do this?

Most people start without real estate experience. The support is built around the parts beginners should not guess on: underwriting, entity structure, lender and CPA intros, tax strategy, landlord conversations, and negotiation support.

I work full time. When do I do this?

Plan for a heavier setup phase and a lighter operating phase. The source material frames the first property at roughly 3 to 5 hours a week during setup, then about 1 hour a week once the cleaner, pricing, messaging, and guest systems are in place.

How much to start?

Bridge is generally the lower-capital path: $10K to $25K for deposit, first month's rent, furnishing, and runway. Ownership depends on the property and loan structure, with $50K to $150K as a typical range. Concierge requires more because the team is sourcing, underwriting, coordinating acquisition, and helping with launch.

My city has Airbnb rules.

Your home city is not the only option. The program material includes remote setup, market selection, regulation checks, mid-term rental options, cleaners, handymen, keyless locks, cameras, and automated guest messaging. Bring the city you're worried about and we'll pressure-test it.

My partner isn't convinced.

Have them join the call so you both hear the same details at the same time.

Is this a sales call?

If the program fits, your advisor will say so. Either way, you'll leave knowing whether this works for you.

What does the team actually help with?

Bridge support can include landlord pre-approach, deal review, lease negotiation support, scripts, roleplay, reference calls, listing setup, dynamic pricing, PMS setup, calendar sync, cleaner SOPs, and guest messaging. Ownership and Concierge support can include buy box definition, sourcing, underwriting, financing path, lender and CPA introductions, entity setup, direct booking site, and performance reviews after launch.

What if I do the work and still get stuck?

That's what the coaching and program guardrails are for. We'll cover the details on the call.

What if landlords won't say yes?

Landlord yes starts with the right target list and a better pitch. Market targeting, pre-approach, scripts, objection handling, coach roleplay, and reference support all make the conversation more professional. The goal is to present a partnership, not beg for permission.

What happens after the call?

If it makes sense, onboarding starts, your STR coach is assigned, and the first plan is built around your route. Bridge starts with market and landlord targets. Ownership and Concierge start with buy box, sourcing, underwriting, and the path to closing. Launch support covers listing buildout, pricing, cleaners, messaging, and early performance checks.
